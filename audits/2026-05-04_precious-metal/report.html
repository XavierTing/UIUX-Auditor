Executive Summary

The Precious Metal flow is **visually polished and functionally complete for a happy path**, but it is **materially under-disclosed for a regulated investment product**. The most consequential issues are not aesthetic — they are economic and regulatory. Pricing is opaque (a single "Indicative Price" hides the bid/ask spread, fee, and FX rate; a *different* indicative price gets executed than the one shown on the chart), risk is invisible until it is too late (no risk panel, no Key Fact Sheet, no past-performance disclaimer near the chart, no allocated-vs-unallocated custody statement), and the entire failure / pending / maker-checker state matrix is undesigned despite a corporate-banking context that strongly implies maker-checker workflow. Visually the product reads as conventional and trustworthy, but trust signals fail closer inspection: the disclaimer on the Review screen is plain grey body text (visually weaker than the marketing chip beside it), T&C consent is passive copy not a checkbox, and the disclaimer references a button label ("Submit") that doesn't exist on the page (the actual button reads "Confirm Buy/Sell Order"). Buy and Sell paths have visibly drifted (different field-labels for the same value, "Show available balance" only present on one path), and Order History is a dead-end (rows are not clickable, no order-detail screen exists). **Per-persona scores:** - **Novice:** **3.5 / 10** — opaque pricing, hidden risk, ambiguous terminology will lead to misinterpretation and complaint. - **Seasoned:** **5.0 / 10** — fast and clean to operate, but missing the disclosure surface (bid/ask, fees, custody, status, drill-down) seasoned investors expect. - **Combined:** **4.0 / 10** — adequate scaffolding, critical gaps in the regulated-domain disclosures and workflow integrity that must be closed before launch. **Overall UX Health Score: 4.0 / 10** — *do not ship until the four critical pricing-transparency, risk-disclosure, maker-checker, and missing-state findings are resolved.*

Feedback & System Status CRITICAL The global header shows a 'Tasks and Statuses' pill (implying maker-checker workflow common in corporate banking), yet no 'submitted', 'pending approval', 'awaiting checker', or 'rejected' state is designed in any flow. #6 Buy Form (Empty) 5. Component & Interaction Design CRITICAL No error / inline-validation, insufficient-balance, market-closed, price-stale, network-failure, or order-rejected states are designed in any of the 14 frames despite multiple form fields with min/max constraints and a real-money trade-execution context. Design the full state matrix before launch: field-level validation (min/max, decimals, account-currency mismatch); page-level errors (insufficient SGD balance, insufficient XAU holding for sell, market closed, price stale > N seconds, network/API failure, order rejected by checker). Each state needs a recovery action.
#7 Pre-Investment Landing 8. Cognitive Load & Clarity HIGH The single most consequential word in the product — 'Indicative' — is never explained inline. It appears in section headers, table columns, and price tiles, but no tooltip, helper, or glossary tells the user that 'indicative' means non-binding and the executed price may differ. Add an inline info-icon next to every 'Indicative' label opening a tooltip: 'For reference only. The price you transact at is set at the moment of execution and may differ.'
#8 Buy Review 9. Trust & Emotional Design HIGH T&C consent is passive copy ('By clicking Submit you confirm you have read…'), not an explicit checkbox. There is no scroll-to-bottom requirement and no visible link-out to the full T&Cs in-flow. Replace passive copy with an explicit unchecked checkbox: '☐ I have read and agree to the Terms and Conditions and the Key Fact Sheet'. Disable the Confirm CTA until both are checked.
#9 Buy Review Banking-domain — Risk disclosure HIGH The disclaimer copy says 'By clicking Submit, you confirm…' but the actual button is labelled 'Confirm Buy Order' (and 'Confirm Sell Order' on the Sell flow). The reference to 'Submit' breaks the user's mental link between consent and action. Change the disclaimer to reference the actual button label, OR rename the button to 'Submit'. Keep both consistent.
#10 Buy Review Banking-domain — Risk disclosure HIGH No Key Fact Sheet (KFS) link, no suitability statement, and no product-knowledge gate exist anywhere in the flow. Standard regulated-investment requirement is missing. Add a KFS link to the Review screen and to the Pre-Investment Landing card. For first-time precious-metal investors, gate Confirm behind a 5-question suitability check.
#11 Pre-Investment Landing Banking-domain — Risk disclosure HIGH Custody model (allocated vs unallocated) is never disclosed. The tagline 'Invest in gold and silver without holding the physical metal' implies unallocated paper bullion, but the bank's balance-sheet exposure model is never stated. Users cannot tell whether their holding is segregated, what happens on bank insolvency, or whether SDIC applies (it does not). Add a 'Custody and Protection' section to the Pre-Investment Landing and the Holding Details panel: 'Your holding is unallocated paper bullion — a contractual claim on OCBC, not segregated physical metal. Holdings are not SDIC-protected.'
#12 Buy Review Banking-domain — Pricing transparency HIGH GST / tax treatment is never mentioned at point of sale. Singapore investment-precious-metal (IPM) gold ≥99.5% purity is GST-exempt, but silver and non-IPM gold attract GST. Silence at commit creates risk of unexpected tax line on statement. Add a 'GST treatment' line to the Review summary: 'GST: 0.00 SGD (IPM gold is GST-exempt)' or '+9% GST (XX SGD)' as appropriate. Same line should appear in the email confirmation.
#13 Order History Dashboard 1. Information Architecture & Navigation HIGH Order History rows are not clickable — there is no row-level chevron, no 'View' action, and no order-detail screen exists in the deck. Users cannot drill into an order to see breakdown, fees, settlement, or the termsheet. Make every row clickable to an Order Detail screen showing: full economics (price, qty, fee, FX, GST, total), settlement status, termsheet PDF link, and (for corporate) maker/checker history.
#14 Order History Dashboard 7. Feedback & System Status HIGH No 'Status' column on the Order History table. There is no way to distinguish filled / pending / cancelled / awaiting-approval / rejected orders. All visible rows look the same. Add a STATUS column with coloured pills (Filled / Pending Approval / Awaiting Settlement / Cancelled / Rejected) and make it filterable.
#15 Buy Review 4. Colour & Contrast HIGH The risk / T&C disclaimer on the Review screen is rendered in plain ~12 px grey body text with no icon, no border, no background tint. It is visually weaker than the Buy chip, the summary band, and the dark Confirm button. For a regulated-investment commit screen, this is a serious trust signal gap. Wrap the disclaimer in a tinted panel with a left border (e.g. amber #FEF3C7 / #F59E0B) and an icon, immediately above the Confirm CTA. Add the explicit T&C checkbox from Finding #8.
#16 Order History Dashboard 3. Typography & Readability HIGH Numerical columns (QUANTITY, INDICATIVE PRICE, TOTAL) are left-aligned and use proportional (not tabular) lining figures. Decimals do not line up vertically; finance-table convention requires right-alignment with tabular figures so digit-place comparison is possible. Right-align all numeric columns. Use tabular lining figures (CSS: font-variant-numeric: tabular-nums). Apply same rule to the Holdings Details panel and price tiles.
#17 Buy Form (Filled) 2. Visual Hierarchy & Layout HIGH Primary CTAs ('Review Buy Order', 'Confirm Buy Order', 'Create Alert') are dark slate (~#3F4A55) — the visually quietest element on the screen. The most important action does not compete with the chart card on the left or the Buy chip at top of the right column. Promote primary CTA to a brand-coloured filled button (OCBC red or a brand-blue) — at minimum, raise contrast and scale slightly. Use slate for secondary 'Back'.
#18 Buy Form (Filled) Banking-domain — Pricing transparency HIGH There is no price-validity window communicated. Users cannot tell whether the indicative price shown at form-fill is the same as at confirmation, and slippage between Review and Confirm is invisible. Either (a) lock the quote on Review with a visible countdown (e.g. 'Price held for 30s'), or (b) re-quote on Confirm with explicit 'Price has updated — confirm new price?' interrupt.
#19 Buy Form (Filled) Banking-domain — Risk disclosure HIGH No 'Past performance is not indicative of future results' disclaimer is shown near the price chart on Buy/Sell forms or on the Price Alert form. Standard regulated-finance boilerplate is missing wherever a chart is rendered. Add the boilerplate immediately under every chart: 'Past performance is not indicative of future results. Precious metal prices can fall as well as rise.'
#20 Buy Form (Filled) 2. Visual Hierarchy & Layout MEDIUM On the Buy/Sell form, the eye lands on the Gold (XAU) price card on the left first because it is the largest white-card-with-bold-numbers on the screen. The form on the right (the user's actual task) is visually quieter than the chart. Reduce the chart card visual weight (smaller header, less saturated chart fill) and elevate the form (e.g. subtle elevation/shadow or accent border at the top). Or invert the columns so the form is left-anchored.
#21 Sell Form (Filled) 8. Cognitive Load & Clarity MEDIUM Buy form labels the SGD field 'Investment Amount'; Sell form labels the same SGD field 'Market Value'. The Sell email then reverts to 'Investment Amount' for what was 'Market Value' in-app. Same value, three different labels across the journey. Pick one label and use it everywhere. Recommend 'Proceeds (SGD)' for Sell flows and 'Cost (SGD)' for Buy flows — both more accurate than the current labels and unambiguous between the two paths.
#22 Buy Form (Filled) 5. Component & Interaction Design MEDIUM The swap icon between 'Investment Amount' and 'Quantity to Buy' fields is unlabelled. Bi-directional auto-calculation (typing one fills the other) is implicit; novices won't know to use it. Add a tooltip on the swap icon: 'Quantity and amount are linked — fill either, the other will calculate automatically.' Or remove the swap and auto-fill the inactive field on blur.
#23 Sell Form (Filled) 6. Forms & Data Entry MEDIUM The 'Show available balance' link appears under the Credit Account on the filled Buy form, but is missing from the empty Buy form (where users would benefit before typing) and entirely absent from the Sell form. Either the Sell flow needs an equivalent 'Show available holdings' link, or the Buy/Sell paths have visibly drifted. Show 'Available balance: X SGD' inline under Credit Account on the Buy form (empty + filled). Show 'Available holding: X ounces' inline under the metal account on the Sell form. Same component, mirrored.
#24 Sell Form (Empty) 6. Forms & Data Entry MEDIUM Min/max guardrails are asymmetric. Buy form shows a Minimum (24.59 SGD, 0.01 ounces) but no Maximum (no exposure cap, no available-balance hard-stop label). Sell form shows a Maximum (25.86 ounces, 158,484.99 SGD) but no Minimum. Users cannot tell at a glance what the full bounds of each field are. Show both Min and Max for every numeric field on both forms. Format: 'Min 0.01 oz · Max 25.86 oz'.

Top 5 Priority Recommendations

Make pricing transparent end-to-end

What to fix: Today, a single "Indicative Price" hides the spread, fee, FX, and GST. The user sees one number on the chart (6,581.85 SGD/oz) and is silently transacted at a different one (6,128.50 SGD/oz on the order history). Buy and Sell trade off the same number.
Why it matters: This is the single biggest mis-sell risk in the product. Novices feel deceived; seasoned investors trade elsewhere; compliance is exposed. (Findings #1, #2, #3, #12, #18.)
How to fix it: On every chart, show **two** quotes: "Buy at X.XX SGD · Sell at Y.YY SGD" with the spread visible. On Review, break the cost into a 5-line table: `Unit price × Quantity = Subtotal | ± Fee | ± FX adjustment | ± GST | = Total`. Lock the quote on Review with a visible countdown timer ("Quote valid for 30s — refresh"). Rename the chart price "Live indicative" and the executed price "Executed price" so they cannot be confused.
Effort estimate: **Large Effort** — pricing service must publish bid/ask separately; Review template must add the breakdown; quote-lock logic is new.

Surface risk and disclosure at decision time, not in a collapsed FAQ

What to fix: All risk content lives inside a collapsed FAQ ("Things to look out for") at the bottom of the landing. The Review-screen disclaimer is grey body copy with no border or icon. There is no Key Fact Sheet, no suitability gate, no past-performance boilerplate near charts, and no statement of custody (allocated vs unallocated) or SDIC coverage.
Why it matters: Regulated-investment products require risk visibility before commitment. Today's design buries every signal. (Findings #4, #8, #10, #11, #15, #19.)
How to fix it: Add a persistent **Risk panel** above every chart on Buy/Sell forms with three lines: *"Precious metal prices can fall as well as rise. Past performance is not indicative of future results. Your holding is unallocated paper bullion — a contractual claim on OCBC, not SDIC-insured."* On the Review screen, replace passive consent copy with an explicit checkbox: *"☐ I have read and agree to the Terms and Conditions and the Key Fact Sheet"*; disable Confirm until both are checked; render the disclaimer in a tinted amber panel with an icon. Add a KFS link on the landing card and on Review.
Effort estimate: **Medium Lift** — content + new component, but no new pricing logic.

Design the missing state matrix and the maker-checker workflow

What to fix: Across 14 frames, no error, validation, insufficient-balance, market-closed, price-stale, network-failure, or order-rejected state exists. The global header shows "Tasks and Statuses" implying maker-checker — yet no Submitted / Pending Approval / Awaiting Settlement / Rejected state is designed.
Why it matters: A real-money trading flow with no failure modes is a launch blocker. For corporate banking, maker-checker is the *fundamental* operational pattern. (Findings #5, #6, #14.)
How to fix it: Catalogue the full state matrix and design each one: field-level validation, page-level errors (insufficient balance, insufficient holding, market closed, price stale > N seconds, network/API failure), order-rejected with reason. Add four maker-checker states (Submitted / Pending Approval / Approved-Pending Settlement / Rejected) — surfaced as row badges on Order History, as the acknowledgement variant after Confirm ("Order submitted for approval"), and as a status row on the order-detail screen.
Effort estimate: **Large Effort** — net-new screens and a workflow integration.

Make Order History the single source of truth — and make rows drillable

What to fix: Today's Order History is a static list. Rows are not clickable, there is no Status column, and no order-detail screen exists in the deck.
Why it matters: Corporate users need to retrieve termsheets, reconcile statements, and audit trades. Without drill-down, the product has no audit trail. (Findings #13, #14.)
How to fix it: Add a Status column with coloured pills (Filled / Pending Approval / Awaiting Settlement / Cancelled / Rejected). Make every row clickable to an Order Detail screen showing the full economic breakdown (price, qty, fee, FX, GST, total), settlement status, termsheet PDF download, and maker/checker history. Right-align all numeric columns and use tabular figures.
Effort estimate: **Medium Lift** — Order Detail screen is new; rest is incremental.

Reconcile Buy and Sell — same value, same name, same component

What to fix: Buy form labels the SGD field "Investment Amount"; Sell form calls the same field "Market Value"; the Sell email reverts to "Investment Amount". "Show available balance" appears on the Buy form (filled state only) but is missing from the empty state and from the Sell form. Min/max guardrails differ asymmetrically (Buy: min only; Sell: max only).
Why it matters: Buy and Sell are mirrored journeys for the same user; inconsistencies erode trust and make muscle memory transfer poorly. (Findings #21, #23, #24.)
How to fix it: Adopt one label each per direction — "Cost (SGD)" for Buy and "Proceeds (SGD)" for Sell — and use them in-app and in-email. Add an "Available balance / Available holding" inline read on both empty and filled states for both forms (mirrored component). Show both Min and Max on every numeric field on both forms.
Effort estimate: **Quick Win** — copy and component-prop changes, no logic.

What's Working Well

Conventional Buy/Sell colour mapping is followed.

Buy = green, Sell = red, gain = green up-arrow, loss = red down-arrow. Internally consistent and matches Western finance convention. Helps both personas orient quickly.

Acknowledgement screens are clean and emotionally well-pitched.

Green check + concise success message + drill-back-to-overview link + drill-into-order CTA is the right pattern; avoids over-celebration of what is in fact a routine financial transaction.

Email confirmations are well-structured and tonally appropriate.
